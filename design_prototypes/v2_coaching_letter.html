II. The Table — Woodruff Protocol, Both Riders

On the bike (every ride past ninety minutes): 60–80 g carbohydrate per hour beginning at minute 15, on a twenty-minute timer; 600–1,000 mg sodium per hour; 20–24 oz fluid per hour.

Off the bike: protein at every meal; a modest deficit on easy and rest days only — never on long-ride days; carbohydrate the evening before every long ride. For Robert, body composition remains the single largest lever: each kilogram is worth roughly five meters per hour of climbing speed — ten to twelve minutes across the race, free of charge.

III. Seven Weeks, Week by Week

July 20 – 26 Re-entry

Jonathan: Z2 only, no threshold anywhere. The long ride rebuilds to 4:00 Saturday in Park City. Two lifts. The gate — resting HR under 66, sleep past seven hours — governs everything.

Robert: Recover from the back-to-back block. Base under 128, one long ride 3.5–4 h under 133. The FTP field test happens the moment the legs are fresh; the watch's FTP setting gets corrected regardless.

July 27 – August 2 Build I

Jonathan: First big-vert week — 5.5–6 h and ~8,000 ft, climbs at or under HR 145. One threshold touch (2×12 at 150–158) only if genuinely fresh.

Robert: The FTP re-test, if not already done. One tempo session (3–4 × 8–10 min at 138–144). The long ride reaches 4.5–5 h, fueled on the timer.

August 3 – 9 Build II

Jonathan: Park City big-vert; the first cool-Utah long ride. Race-band climbing, full fuel rehearsal.

Robert: The long climbing ride stretches to 5–6 h, kept aerobic — durability is his gift; this week leans into it. Punch budget drilled on every climb.

August 10 – 16 Down Week

Jonathan: Memphis engine at reduced volume, everything easy.

Robert: The scheduled recovery week — at sixty, this is where the fitness is actually banked. Sleep is the workout.

August 17 – 23 The Peak

Both, together: the dress rehearsal — the year's biggest ride at full race protocol. Jonathan: pacing bands and the aid-station swap, rehearsed. Robert: 165–180 W on every climb, fuel on the timer, ceiling honored from the first mile to the last.

August 24 – 30 Taper

Jonathan: Load down ~40%; short openers. No new fitness is available now — only freshness. Last lift a week out.

Robert: Volume down, two or three short climb touches to stay sharp. Sleep protected; no weight-cutting in the final fortnight.

August 31 – September 6 Race Week

Both: light openers Tuesday and Thursday; the carbohydrate load Thursday through Friday. Saturday, September 5: fuel from minute 15. Jonathan rides the first half at 165–180 W and HR ≤145. Robert climbs at ≤135, spends fewer than five matches an hour, and rides his own race to the finish he is built for.

Easy days truly easy · fuel from minute fifteen · neither man chases the other uphill.
