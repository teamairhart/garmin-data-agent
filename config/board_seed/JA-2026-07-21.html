What the ride actually was

Prescribed: 2:00 Z2, HR ≤138, ride by the cap and let watts fall in the heat. Executed: 2:15:46, 29.7 mi, 42% true Z2 / 51% upper-aero / 7% at-or-over threshold, in 86 °F rising to 93. The first 2:06 was ridden at just 94 W — and HR still couldn't hold the cap. The quarter-by-quarter tells it plainly:

Power falling while HR climbs at trivial wattage is the fatigue signature, not a heat story: five days ago in identical 86 °F, the same roads gave 123 W at HR 134. Today ~30 W less bought 6 bpm more.

The trend markers — worst of the entire log

  • HR@120W: 141 — the hot-Memphis band runs 131–142, and this tops it (previous worst 142 came at higher intensity on a harder day).
  • EF 0.91 — lowest recorded; matched-power decoupling 12.5% — highest recorded.
  • A subtlety worth knowing: the simple first-half/second-half decoupling read 2.0% and looks clean — it's misleading. The hard finish raised second-half power together with HR, flattering the ratio. The matched-power method (comparing HR at the same wattage early vs late) shows the honest 12.5%. When the two disagree, trust matched-power.
Verdict: not fitness loss — Sunday's 15-set leg session plus Monday's tempo experiment, presented as data. This is the KPI gate (RHR <66, sleep ≥7 h) doing exactly what it exists to do, one day late.

What to work on

  • Every second above HR 165 sat in the final 9 minutes: four bursts, closing with 5 min at 162 W and a 600 W sprint to HR 182 — on legs that had refused 94 W all morning, in a week whose single rule is no threshold. The fatigue wasn't controllable today; the finish was.
  • The morning brief said "wrap early if HR won't stay down at ~120 W." That condition was met by minute 40. The honest move was 90 minutes and done — duration is not the metric that matters in a re-entry week, aerobic time under the cap is.

What went well

  • Duration delivered (2:15 vs 2:00) and the ride was genuinely easy by power (IF 0.54, TSS 66) — the aerobic cost of the day was modest, which is why this stays an off-profile note rather than a real setback.
  • Riding at 94 W instead of forcing 130+ was the right instinct — the body set the ceiling and the watts respected it. That's the heat/fatigue protocol working.
