How the day was spent

56% of the ride was climbing — 112 minutes at 194 W average — against 39 min descending and 34 min flat. Total work 1,603 kJ on 3,448 ft of gain. For context, that's more time-at-climbing-power than most riders log in a week, done the day after a 1,228 kJ mountain ride.

His compressed HR zones (118–145 is the whole range) physically cannot resolve climbing effort, which is exactly why the new pedals change his racing: HR could never tell him the difference between sustainable and too hot on a climb. Watts can.

What went well

  • Zero seconds over threshold across 3.3 hours; max HR 142. On a bad day, the discipline never cracked.
  • Descents 76% coasted with HR dropping to ~100 — he uses every descent as genuine recovery, which over a 9-hour race is worth real minutes.
  • The cross-day fatigue check is the quiet headline: climbing HR at a matched 120–170 W was 134.4 Sunday → 133.1 Monday. Back-to-back 1,200+ kJ mountain days at age 60 and the cardiovascular system showed no strain carryover. The engine is durable; whatever felt bad, it wasn't the heart.

What to work on

  • 8 punches ≥247 W. At his profile each surge over ~130% of threshold is deep anaerobic — a match burned that the diesel then has to pay back. They cluster at pitch changes and technical moves; the fixes are pre-shifting before the pitch, carrying momentum into it, and letting speed sag rather than stomping. Race target: fewer than 5 per hour in the first half.
  • Bad legs + healthy engine = the under-fueling picture. When HR stays available but the legs go flat, glycogen is the usual culprit — and fueling was already priority #1 from the recon. 60–80 g carb/hr from minute 15, on a 20-minute timer, every ride past 90 minutes. His durability masks under-fueling until it's late; that's precisely why it needs the timer, not feel.
  • Steep-climb cadence (63–70 rpm) is gear-pinned, not a habit. Median development on ≥6% pitches was 1.3–1.5 m per crank revolution — bottom-gear territory at his 3.7–4.4 mph climbing speeds. "Spin more" isn't available without easier gearing or more watts, so: check the cassette (one easier gear ≈ +7 rpm on the steeps), and do cadence work on moderate grades where 75–80 rpm is actually attainable.
