The headline — the lab FTP can't survive this file

First ride ever on the Assioma pedals, and it immediately re-rates him. Across 63 minutes of accumulated climbing he averaged 207 W at HR 134 — eleven beats under his lab threshold HR — including a 10-minute climb at 219 W (2.30 W/kg), his hardest sustained effort on record. Holding 97–115% of your FTP for that long at sub-threshold heart rate is physiologically impossible; the December figure of 190 W is stale. The real number is likely 210–220 W, which moves him from 2.0 to ~2.2+ W/kg — seven months of training, now measurable.

What went well

  • Disciplined base day where it counted: avg HR 126 (mid-P2), fourth consecutive ride with zero HR drift (126→126 across 2.6 h). The durability that finished P2P 2025 without cramping is fully intact.
  • Long climbs (≥10 min) averaged 194 W vs 164 W on short ones — he's stronger long than short, the reverse of most riders and exactly the profile P2P rewards.

What to work on

  • 14 punches ≥247 W plus 15% of ride time in P4 — hotter than a base day needs, and nearly double Monday's punch count on a shorter ride. Same prescription: pre-shift, momentum, let speed sag on pitch changes.
  • Recorded max HR 175 is a sensor artifact — a 68-bpm jump inside one minute at minute 70, physiologically impossible at any age. True max on the day was ~151. Worth checking whether the watch was reading wrist HR (notoriously spiky on rough MTB descents) vs. a chest strap; if there's no strap in the loop, one would clean up his HR data considerably.
