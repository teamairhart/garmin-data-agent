# Maximum Overload Notes

These notes are based on public descriptions and interviews, not the full copyrighted book text.

## What The Public Sources Say

- `Bicycling Maximum Overload for Cyclists` is by Jacques DeVore and Roy Wallack.
- The public publisher description presents it as a strength-based program designed to improve speed, endurance, and durability while reducing how much riding time is needed.
- A public interview with Jacques DeVore describes a progression that starts with movement assessment, then a force-production phase, and then "mini-sets" built around repeated high-output efforts with short rest.

## Practical Takeaways For This Project

From the public material alone, the pieces most likely to matter for this athlete are:

- stronger force production for steep climbing and repeated accelerations
- better whole-body durability for long trail events
- improved late-race stability and fatigue resistance
- mobility and movement screening before heavy loading

## Conservative Integration Plan

Until the exact Dr. Testa workout prescriptions and the actual strength routine are entered into the repo, use these as provisional rules:

- Start with `2` gym sessions per week if recovery is good and key bike sessions stay intact.
- Reduce to `1` maintenance session during very high bike-load weeks or close to priority races.
- Keep heavy lower-body work away from the most important threshold and long-ride days.
- Prioritize movement quality, trunk stability, hip control, and posterior-chain strength before chasing load.
